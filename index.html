Overview

Story Overview

Joan of the City reimagines opera as a spatial, walkable experience. The audience moves through real space while light, voice, shadow, and communion appear around them through XR glasses — Joan's story retold across a sequence of staged scenes.

The core experience

Audience members, wearing XR glasses, experience a series of scenes — Light, Voice, Shadow, and Communion — each with its own visual language, audio, and choreography of virtual elements anchored to the real environment. Ravens flock and land; particles carry libretto text; a presence rises and levitates in Communion. The work is performed live, with cues driven scene by scene.

💡

North star: the virtual elements must feel anchored and intentional — a raven that separates from the flock, approaches Joan, and acts with apparent compassion reads as a character, not an effect.

Overview

Pillars

Four commitments shape every creative and technical decision.

📍

Anchored, not floating

Content holds its place in the real world — Anchor behavior, not Follow. Stable across devices and across a performance.

🎶

Audio-reactive

Light, particles, and Communion respond to the score. The sound and the visuals move together.

🎭

Character, not effect

The Hero Raven, the Shadow, the Communion presence — each behaves with intention, scripted through real animation states.

🛟

Safe & consistent

Cues fire predictably across every headset, and STOP ALL can return the show to a safe state instantly.
