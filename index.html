Overview

Story Overview

Joan of the City reimagines opera as a spatial, walkable experience. The audience becomes the chorus; the city becomes the stage; XR glasses dissolve the boundary between the score and the street.

The core experience

A single audience member — or a synchronized group — moves through a route in the real city. Through their XR glasses, virtual performers, light, text, and architecture appear anchored to real locations. Music and voice are spatialized: a duet might rise from a balcony that is physically empty. Joan's story is told in movements, each tied to a place.

💡

North star: a stranger watching from across the street should sense that something is happening — even without glasses — because the performers, sound, and audience movement make it real.

Overview

Pillars

Four commitments shape every creative and technical decision.

🌍

Site-specific

Every scene belongs to a real location. The world reacts to where you stand.

🎶

Music-led

The score drives timing. Visuals and show-control follow the conductor's clock.

👁️

Diegetic AR

UI is part of the world. No floating menus during a movement — the opera never breaks character.

🤝

Shared presence

Multiple audience members see a consistent world, co-located in the same scene.
