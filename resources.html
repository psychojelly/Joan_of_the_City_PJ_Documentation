Glossary

Shared vocabulary keeps design and engineering aligned. Use these terms in code, in tickets, and in the rehearsal room.

TermMeaning
MovementA self-contained scene of the opera, bound to a location and a section of the score.
AnchorA persistent real-world point (geo or spatial) that virtual content is pinned to.
BeatThe smallest directed unit of action, triggered by the conductor clock or audience position.
CueOne action attached to a beat — spawn, move, light, audio, retire.
Conductor ClockThe master timeline that synchronizes music, performers, and show-control.
PhantomA virtual performer rendered in AR (vocalist, chorus member, or apparition).
Stage VolumeThe mapped bounds of a real location where a movement is performed.
Show-ControlThe system that cues lights, audio, and phantoms in time with the score.
Running OrderThe full ordered list of beats that makes up a performance.

Contributing & reviews

How we work together in the repo. Small, reviewable changes keep a complex show stable.

Branch & commit

  • Branch from main using feat/<movement>-<short-desc> or fix/<area>-<short-desc>.
  • Commit in present tense: add phantom rig to movement II.
  • Never commit large binaries outside LFS-tracked paths.

Pull requests

  • Keep PRs scoped to one movement or one system where possible.
  • Include a short clip or screenshot of the change in-headset or in-simulator.
  • Update the relevant doc page in the same PR.
  • At least one engineering review + one design review for anything that touches a staged movement.
🔒

Scene merges are dangerous. Unity scene & prefab files conflict badly. Coordinate before two people edit the same .unity file, and prefer prefab-based composition over giant scenes.
