Glossary

Shared vocabulary keeps design and engineering aligned. Use these terms in code, in tickets, and in the rehearsal room.

TermMeaning
SceneA self-contained part of the show — Light, Voice, Shadow, Communion, and the new Joan of the Real / Hustle / Ground.
CueA single directed moment within a scene; one row in the cue sheet.
Cue sheetThe master list of cues — asset, AR/visual imagery, audio, and performer action.
Anchor vs FollowAnchor pins content to a fixed real-world point; Follow tracks the headset. Target behavior is Anchor.
STOP ALLThe master safety — halts and resets every running cue at once.
Hero RavenThe single raven that separates from the flock, approaches Joan, acts intentionally, and remains after the flock leaves.
CollageTear-off / fly-off transition and thunder-triggered sky text in the Voice scene.
CommunionThe scene where a presence emerges, rises, and levitates over ground ripples (~12′ radius).
Particle / librettoParticle systems that carry libretto text and data.
T-poseThe neutral pose source from which custom silhouettes and skins are built.
Show-ControlThe operator side that drives cues during a performance.

Contributing & reviews

How we work together in the repo. Small, reviewable changes keep a complex show stable.

Branch & commit

  • Branch from main using feat/<scene>-<short-desc> or fix/<area>-<short-desc>.
  • Commit in present tense: add hero raven states to voice.
  • Never commit large binaries outside LFS-tracked paths.

Pull requests

  • Keep PRs scoped to one scene or one subsystem where possible.
  • Include a short clip or screenshot of the change in-headset or in-simulator.
  • Update the relevant doc page in the same PR.
  • At least one engineering review + one design review for anything that touches a live scene.
🔒

Scene merges are dangerous. Unity scene & prefab files conflict badly. Coordinate before two people edit the same .unity file, and prefer prefab-based composition over giant scenes.
