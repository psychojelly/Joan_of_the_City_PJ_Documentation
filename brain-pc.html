Brain PC & XREAL Glasses

How the headset hardware actually runs the show: the XREAL glasses display and track, while a host “brain” PC does the thinking — running the Unity build, holding the synced assets, and receiving cues.

✅

Grounded in the project. The cue, OSC, and XREAL details below were verified against the Unity project (joan-of-the-city-xreal). A couple of operational details — asset sync and PuTTY — aren't evidenced in the repo yet and stay marked (to confirm).

Brain PC

The two halves

XREAL glasses are display-and-sensor devices — they show images and report head movement, but they don't run the experience on their own. The experience runs on a host machine, the brain PC.

🕶️

XREAL glasses

Show what the brain renders and feed head tracking back. Worn by the audience.

🧠

Brain PC

Runs the Unity build, stores the synced project assets, and receives cues. The compute behind each pair of glasses.

Brain PC

What moves where

Four flows run through the brain PC during a show.

🖼️

Render & tracking

The brain renders the scene and sends it to the glasses; the glasses send head pose back so content stays anchored.

🎬

Cues

The operator fires cues over OSC; the Unity build on the brain receives them and runs the moment. See OSC control system.

📦

Asset sync

New or changed assets are synced onto the brain so its build has the latest content.

🛠️

Management

Terminal access to start/stop the build, check status, or move files.

Brain PC

What runs on the brain

The brain PC runs the Unity build (Unity 6, URP, XREAL SDK) and listens for two streams of OSC cues that drive the show. Both arrive at UDP :7000 through proxy.py.

Two cue streams

StreamOSC addressWhat it does
Visual cues/cue + integerToggles a mapped scene element on/off (via SimpleMessageReceiver).
Audio cues/audio/cue, /audio/jump, /audio/seek, /audio/volDrives the JoanAudio stem engine — play, jump, seek, volume, and StopAll.

Visual cue map (current build)

CueElement
1Video quad locked to camera view
2First Signs — droplets + floor ripple VFX
3Water splash (31-frame mesh sequence)
4Joan's crown (SDF particle system)
5Communion bodies + audio-reactive VFX + BGM
6–8Unassigned — would throw a NullReference if triggered
🔊

STOP ALL is implemented on the audio side (StemAction.StopAll — "stop all" / "kill all" fades every stem to 0). Visual cues are individual toggles; a single global visual STOP ALL would live in the still-open cue slots.

Open questions

What the code doesn't answer — the operational and multi-user pieces still to pin down.

  • Glasses ↔ brain cabling — the exact tether/host mode for the XREAL hardware.
  • One brain per headset, or shared? — the project runs as “multi-user,” but how many brain PCs per show and how they relate isn't in the repo.
  • Cross-device consistency — how multiple headsets/brains stay in sync (the OSC host is a single hardcoded IP today).
  • Asset sync — which tool/folders and manual vs automatic (no sync tooling found in the project).
  • PuTTY's role — terminal and/or file sync to the brain (not evidenced in the repo).
  • How the build is launched — manually, on boot, or remotely on each brain.
  • Anchoring on XREAL — plane detection + recenter handlers exist; how anchors hold long-term ties to the anchoring priority.
